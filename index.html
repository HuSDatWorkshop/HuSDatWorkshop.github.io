Human-Centered Study of Data Science Work Practices

Workshop at CHI 2019 (status: proposal in review) 

ABOUT

With the rise of big data, there has been an increasing need to understand who is working in data science, and how they are doing their work. HCI and CSCW researchers have begun to examine these questions. In this workshop, we invite researchers to share their observations, experiences, hypotheses, and insights, in the hopes of developing a taxonomy of work practices and open issues in the behavioral and social study of data science and data science workers.

CALL FOR SUBMISSIONS

To get involved with the Human-Centered Study of Data Science Work Practices workshop, please submit a position paper following the CHI 2018 format instructions

Topics of interest include (but are not limited to):

  • Contextualization and understanding of data science work practices
  • Studies of the work practices of data scientists, including programming, ideation, and collaboration
  • Supporting the incorporation of ethics and human value stakes into data science work, eg. helping data scientists design for algorithmic fairness, or helping data scientists explore or remedy possible biases in their underlying dataset
  • Supporting policymakers to effectively and appropriately set rules for (what is often highly technical and nuanced) data science work
  • Widening the audience for data science beyond highly technically skilled programmers, such as envisioning ways to bridge the technical gap to better incorporate UX designers and other stakeholders into a data-driven project
  • Widening the audience for data science to novice programmers or end-user programmers, such as helping with wide knowledge gaps novices may have around statistical practice or data work
